from fpdf import FPDF

# Create instance of FPDF class

# Add a page
pdf.add_page()

# Set title
pdf.set_font("Arial", 'B', 16)
pdf.cell(200, 10, "Computer Science Career Path - Guidance and Recommendations", ln=True, align='C')

# Add a line break
pdf.ln(10)

# Set content font
pdf.set_font("Arial", size=12)

     9. **Game Development**: Building video games using specialized engines and frameworks.
     10. **Blockchain Development**: Developing decentralized applications using blockchain technology.
     11. **Database Management**: Working with databases, including relational and NoSQL databases.
     12. **Computer Vision**: Enabling computers to interpret visual data from the world (images, videos).
     13. **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: Working with language data to build systems like chatbots, translation tools.
     14. **Quantum Computing**: Computing using principles of quantum mechanics, an emerging field in CS.
     15. **Robotics**: Combining AI, mechanical engineering, and hardware to create robots and automated systems.
     16. **Computational Biology**: Applying computer science to biological data analysis.

    ),
    ("2. Sort it from easy to tough and duration needed to be expert in each.",
     """
     Based on the learning curve and the estimated time to become an expert in each domain, here’s a ranking from easiest to toughest:
     
     1. **Full Stack Development** - Duration to Expertise: 1.5 - 2 years
     2. **Mobile App Development** - Duration to Expertise: 1.5 - 2 years
     3. **Data Analytics** - Duration to Expertise: 1 - 1.5 years
     4. **Cybersecurity (Basic Level)** - Duration to Expertise: 1.5 - 2 years
     5. **Game Development** - Duration to Expertise: 2 - 3 years
     6. **Database Management & Administration** - Duration to Expertise: 2 - 3 years
     7. **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** - Duration to Expertise: 3 - 4 years
     8. **Machine Learning (ML)** - Duration to Expertise: 3 - 4 years
     9. **Data Science** - Duration to Expertise: 3 - 5 years
     10. **Cloud Computing** - Duration to Expertise: 3 - 4 years
     11. **Blockchain** - Duration to Expertise: 2 - 3 years
     12. **Robotics** - Duration to Expertise: 4 - 5 years
     13. **Computer Graphics and Visualization** - Duration to Expertise: 3 - 4 years
     14. **Computational Biology & Bioinformatics** - Duration to Expertise: 4 - 5 years
     15. **Quantum Computing** - Duration to Expertise: 5 - 7 years
     """
    ),
    ("3. When I graduate in 2027, I need a job. Which one of these skills will land me a job with a decent package and job security of at least 5 years?",
     """
     By 2027, several skills will be in high demand and offer both competitive salaries and job security for at least 5 years. Here's a breakdown of the most promising skills:
     
     1. **Artificial Intelligence (AI) & Machine Learning (ML)**: These fields are expected to see massive growth, particularly in industries such as healthcare, finance, and autonomous vehicles. AI/ML experts will be highly sought after, earning **$100K–$150K/year**.

     7. **Robotics**: As automation in industries like manufacturing and healthcare increases, robotics engineers will be in demand. Robotics engineers earn **$80K–$120K/year**.
     
     These fields provide **strong job security**, with increasing salaries as the demand for skilled professionals grows.
     """
    )
]

# Loop through all questions and answers to add them to the PDF
for question, answer in questions_answers:
    pdf.set_font("Arial", 'B', 12)
    pdf.multi_cell(0, 10, question)
    pdf.set_font("Arial", size=12)
    pdf.multi_cell(0, 10, answer)
    pdf.ln(5)

# Save the PDF to a file
output_path = "/mnt/data/CS_Career_Guidance.pdf"
